Do I Need a New Roof?

A roof can naturally reach the end of its useful life without experiencing a roof failure. It may just look old and worn. However, if replacing an old roof is delayed, it could result in much bigger problems down the road. Plus, a new energy efficient roof can lower your monthly energy bill, and greatly increases your home value!

Potential signs that your roof may need to be replaced:

Shingle edges are curled or shingle tabs are cracked or missing

Bald spots on shingles where granules are missing

Your roof is at least 20 years old

Neighbors are getting new roofs. Homes built around the same time period experience the same weather conditions

The roof just looks old and worn

Dark streaks. Algae causes dark lines, and can sometimes signify internal rot

Leaking roof after rain
